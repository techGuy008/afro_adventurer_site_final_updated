Meet the Founder

Built From
Experience.

My father didn’t even own a passport. I became the first in my family to cross an international border — not for migration, like my Peruvian grandparents did, but by choice. And since my first trip, I never stopped trying to open up borders for my community.

I’m an Afro-Latina who chose not to chase the American dream — I built a global one. I’m a pre-med student turned cultural anthropology grad, turned tech Product Leader — twice nominated for the Trailblazer Award (the top honor at Red Hat/IBM) and recognized as Latina in Tech of the Year. A global speaker at top tech venues across the world: USA, Canada, Spain, China, Germany, and more.

I hold a Cultural Anthropology degree with undergraduate research at Princeton and NOAA. A solo traveler for 10+ years across 40+ countries, living in 10+ of them across the years, picking up new cultures and languages in the process. I bring a rare combination of academic rigor, tech credibility, and lived global experience to everything I build.

As a solo female traveler who has survived the highest highs and the lowest lows, I know that having a community is not a nice-to-have. It is a necessity — not just to navigate travel, but career, identity, and life abroad too.
